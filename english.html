Informations about B category

Category B means driving a passenger and cargo motor vehicle whose mass does not exceed 3,500 kg (3.5 t). The vehicle must not have more than 8 seats (not including the driver's seat). With this category, you can tow a trailer with the maximum permissible weight of up to 750 kg on a motor vehicle.

How to get a driver's license

Driving training is an extremely important process. All elements from phase to phase of teaching up to the final exam require the candidate to conscientiously and responsibly prepare and motivate, psychologically support, relax and concentrate to provide a successful result. My experience of over four decades in teaching, theory and practical training, examination of driver candidates, without false modesty, is a guarantee to the candidate that he will learn to drive well and love driving. Also successfully pass a serious driving test. And the essential thing is that he will be a careful beginner, an independent and safe driver.

1. Theoretical teaching

Candidates attend classes where they learn basic traffic rules, regulations and signs. Topics related to traffic safety, basic technical aspects of vehicles and providing first aid in the event of an accident are also covered. This part is crucial for understanding how traffic works and how drivers should behave on the road. The class consists of 40 hours of lectures and exercises, lasts 3 to 4 weeks, is compulsory, and the dates are agreed with the theory lecturer.

2. Passing the theoretical exam

After completing the theoretical classes, candidates take a test that checks their knowledge of traffic rules, signs and regulations. The test consists of multiple-choice questions, and successful passing of this exam enables the transition to practical training. The candidate applies for a free appointment, which suits him, no later than 8 days after attending the theory class. The test is taken at the driving school at the specified test time.

3. Medical examination and First
Aid Course

The candidate chooses a free appointment and schedules his examination, course and First Aid exam at a health facility. A medical certificate is a requirement for starting practical training and passing the driving test.

4. Practical training - Driving

In this phase, candidates start driving with an instructor. They learn the basic techniques of driving a vehicle, including moving, stopping, turning, parking and driving in different traffic situations. The goal is to gain a sense of safety and self-confidence in driving a vehicle. Driving lessons consist of 40 (forty) practical lessons that include:
- training at the training ground,
- training in the settlement,
- training outside the settlement,
- training in night driving conditions,
- training on the routes for passing the practical exam.

5. Test drive and simulations

Before the actual exam, candidates go through test drives and simulations in order to better prepare for real traffic situations. This includes driving in urban and non-urban conditions, driving in different weather conditions and solving complex traffic challenges. After completing the mandatory 40 hours of practical training, the candidate applies for the driving test. In the 8 to 10 days before the practical exam, the candidate has the possibility of additional practical training (number of hours if necessary), on the route routes for taking the exam and at the test site.

6. Taking a practical exam

After completing the training, the candidate takes the practical exam before the commission. During the exam, the candidate's ability to drive the vehicle independently in real traffic conditions, obey traffic regulations and react to different situations on the road is tested. The test consists of 2 parts:
- test at the training ground,
- driving test in the settlement.

7. Obtaining a driver's license

After passing the driving test, the candidate at the driving school receives the documentation required for obtaining a driver's license at the competent organization of the MUP. This stage marks the beginning of the driving experience, with further responsibility for the safety of yourself and other road users.

Practical training - driving

1. phase - 10 to 12 classes of driving
- The first lessons are the teaching of familiarization with the vehicle, basic functions, mechanism and preparation of the vehicle for driving. - The driving instructor gets to know the student-candidate and builds a relationship of trust, respect and responsibility. Then the personality of the new, future driver begins to be built, taking into account the character, temperament, habits and motivation of the student who begins the training.
- In the first phase, the student-candidate learns to properly adjust the vehicle and use it independently.
- Departure, change of gear, use of controls and devices in the vehicle. Vehicle actions.
- Moving, maintaining direction, turning, entering and exiting traffic, and stopping.
- Monitoring the traffic situation, vehicles and pedestrians, traffic signs and markings and light signaling. The candidate is then, as well as later, at the beginning of the lesson highlighted the topic, goal, task, and at the end provides the conclusion of the lesson.
2. phase - from 13. to 30. class of driving
- The student, step by step, becomes independent to use the vehicle in a proper and safe manner, to monitor the traffic situation, to make decisions, according to the rules and regulations, and to drive in a safe manner.
- At the end of this phase, the student should have developed the habits of:
a) correct vehicle settings,
b) safe and proper inclusion and exclusion from traffic,
c) independent and proper driving of the vehicle, controlling both the traffic situation and the devices and equipment in the vehicle itself,
d) reacting and solving all circumstances in traffic, since many participants do not act properly or safely through carelessness, ignorance, irresponsibility,
e) act with care, respect and appreciation of all other traffic participants.
3. phase - last 10 classes of driving
- It is the phase of independent participation in traffic. The candidate carries out the instructions by independently observing and solving traffic situations.
- This phase lasts 10 teaching hours, and it is often necessary to prepare the student to successfully master them with additional teaching and supplementary classes, and then pass the practical driving test. In this phase, it is necessary to build independence and the ability to concentrate on solving the test task, on the training ground and driving in the neighborhood.

Frequently Asked Questions

When can I start category B
training?

At the earliest from the age of 16. Since the candidate can take the driver's test at the earliest at the age of 17, it is preferable to enroll a few months before the age of 17, to attend theoretical classes at a driving school and a First Aid course. After passing the TEST, the candidate undergoes a medical examination and can start practical training. The candidate signs the contract, if he is under 18 years of age, a parent or guardian must sign the consent.

How long does THEORY last?

3 to 4 weeks. Compulsory 40 teaching hours, 3 hours a day, in the morning or afternoon, in agreement with the theory teacher.

How long does PRACTICAL
TRAINING - DRIVING last?

It lasts 4 to 5 weeks. Appointments are agreed with the practical training instructor.

When do I take the exams?

After the completion of the theoretical lessons, the TEST and after the completion of the practical DRIVING training, they are taken in the first time allotted to the driving school for holding the exam. It is a period of 7 to 10 days. During that period, the candidate practices his knowledge for successfully passing the exam.

Additional classes on the routes for the practical exam significantly increase the chance of a successful exam. Every candidate who dedicates himself to training and wants to practice the exam tasks quickly and easily passes the driving test. I really have many such candidates, of all categories, generations and occupations, in decades of education.
